Hi! My name is Dimitri. I have been using and contributing to Open Source
Software for the best part of the last twenty years — as a developer,
maintainer, packager, release manager, software and database architect, and
sometimes *devops*. In the same time frame I started several companies with a
strong Open Source business model, and held *manager* positions at times too,
up to the Executive level.

If you've read [Charity Major's Engineer/Manager
Pendulum](https://charity.wtf/2017/05/11/the-engineer-manager-pendulum/), there
is something to that in my career — from the hacker to the CEO of a small
company, from the PostgreSQL Major Contributor to the CTO of a larger one.

I am a **PostgreSQL Major Contributor**: I wrote the
[Extension Packaging](https://www.postgresql.org/docs/current/extend-extensions.html)
feature and the
[Event Triggers](https://www.postgresql.org/docs/current/event-triggers.html)
facility, among other contributions. As a [Debian](https://www.debian.org)
[maintainer](https://qa.debian.org/developer.php?login=dim@tapoueh.org) I helped
build [apt.postgresql.org](https://wiki.postgresql.org/wiki/Apt) — a repository
of every supported PostgreSQL version for every supported Debian release —
together with [Christoph Berg](https://github.com/ChristophBerg) and
[Magnus Hagander](https://blog.hagander.net).

These days I build open-source tools around PostgreSQL — see my
[Projects](/projects/) and my [Emacs Lisp](/emacs/) packages — and I write here
about getting more out of SQL. A good place to start is
[Understanding Window Functions](/blog/2013/08/understanding-window-functions/),
part of the data-driven [YeSQL](/categories/yesql/) series.
